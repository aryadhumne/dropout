HOW IT WORKS

What is EduDrop?

A digital platform designed to reduce primary school dropouts through early identification and structured intervention.

1

Teachers Update Student Data

Attendance, grades, assignments, and quiz scores are entered into the system regularly by teachers for each student.

2

AI Identifies At-Risk Students

Our prediction engine analyzes patterns and flags students showing early warning signs of dropping out, with explainable reasoning.

3

Principals Review & Verify

School principals get a complete dashboard with analytics, risk trends, and can verify flagged cases before escalation.

4

NGOs Intervene & Support

Verified at-risk cases are forwarded to NGO volunteers who provide counselling, parent meetings, financial aid, and mentorship.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about EduDrop, whether you're a school, teacher, or NGO looking to get started.

EduDrop is an AI-powered platform that helps schools identify students at risk of dropping out. Teachers enter attendance and academic data, and our system automatically flags at-risk students using a scoring algorithm, enabling early intervention by principals and NGOs.

EduDrop supports four roles — Students, Teachers, Principals, and NGO Volunteers. Each role has a dedicated dashboard tailored to their responsibilities, from data entry to monitoring and intervention.

The risk score is based on four factors: monthly test score (below 35 adds 40 points), attendance (below 60% adds 30 points), incomplete assignments (adds 15 points), and poor quiz performance (adds 15 points). A total score above 50 flags the student as high risk.
